I completed a PhD in Philosophy at the University of Edinburgh in 2013, in the School of Philosophy, Psychology and Language Sciences. My thesis is concerned with philosophical issues about cooperation and social cognition. It is entitled Joint action without and beyond planning. Since finishing my PhD, I have continued to do work in this area, as well as in the area of collective responsibility, and philosophy of action more broadly.

As of July 2017, I am a research fellow at the Department of Philosophy at Lund University, where I am working on a project on collective action and collective responsibility. I am a member of the interdisciplinary Metaphysics and Collectivity research group.

Previously, I was a postdoctoral fellow at the Center for Subjectivity Research, Department of Media, Cognition and Communication, University of Copenhagen, from January 2015 to July 2017. January to July 2016, I spent as a Visiting Scholar at the Department of Philosophy, Stanford University.

For my publications, see my academia.edu page or my PhilPapers profile.
